When to Call an Emergency Locksmith

You should call an emergency locksmith any time you cannot access your home, car, or business and cannot wait until regular business hours. Common emergency situations include:

  • Locked out of your house or apartment at night or on a weekend
  • Keys locked inside your car, truck, or SUV
  • A key broken off in your door lock or ignition
  • Lock damaged after an attempted break-in
  • An eviction or need to change locks immediately
  • Child or pet locked inside a vehicle
  • Business lockout preventing you from opening or closing
  • Frozen or jammed lock in extreme weather
⚠ Child or Pet in Vehicle: If a child or pet is locked in a hot vehicle, call 911 immediately before calling a locksmith. Emergency services can respond faster in life-threatening situations.

What ID Do I Need to Show the Locksmith?

For your security and ours, we verify that you have the right to access the property before beginning any work:

  • Home lockout: Government-issued photo ID with the matching address (driver's license, state ID, or passport + utility bill)
  • Car lockout: Driver's license + vehicle registration in your name
  • Apartment lockout: Photo ID + lease agreement or landlord authorization
  • Business lockout: Employee ID, business card, or payroll stub plus ownership documents if changing locks

How to Stay Safe While Waiting for a Locksmith

  • Wait in a well-lit, public area if it's nighttime
  • Stay near a neighbor's home or a lit business
  • Keep your phone charged and the locksmith's number handy
  • Do not leave your property unattended if the door is already open
  • Confirm the locksmith's name and company before allowing entry
  • Ask for their license number before work begins

Avoiding Locksmith Scams in Dallas

Unfortunately, not all locksmiths advertising online are legitimate. Here's how to protect yourself:

  • Call a locksmith you know or have verified in advance — not a random Google ad
  • Ask for a firm quote before work begins — not just a "service fee"
  • Texas requires all locksmiths to carry a TACLA license — ask to see it
  • Beware of prices that seem too low (bait-and-switch is common)
  • Verify the company name matches their vehicle and uniform
