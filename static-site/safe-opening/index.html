Safe Opening Techniques

1. Manipulation

For dial combination locks, a skilled locksmith can "listen" for the fall of the drive cam and wheels using a stethoscope or by feel, determining the combination without any physical intrusion. This leaves the safe completely undamaged. Takes 30–120 minutes depending on the lock.

2. Electronic Bypass

Many electronic keypad safes have override procedures using factory codes, bypass cables, or magnetic keys. If the batteries have died, we can often provide external power to the keypad. If the electronics have failed, we may need to scope the lock.

3. Scoping (Minimal Invasive)

A small hole (typically 1/4″) is drilled in a predetermined location that allows a borescope or scope to view the lock mechanism. The combination is determined visually and the safe opened. The hole is repaired with a hardened steel plug. The safe remains fully functional.
