What Is Lock Rekeying?

Lock rekeying is the process of changing the internal pin configuration of a lock cylinder so that old keys no longer work — without replacing the lock hardware itself. A locksmith removes the cylinder, replaces the driver pins to match a new key pattern, and cuts new keys on-site. The result: all previous keys are invalid, and only your new keys work.

Rekeying vs. Replacing: Rekeying achieves the same security outcome as replacing your locks — at a fraction of the cost. The average homeowner saves $50–$150 per lock by rekeying instead of replacing.

How Rekeying Works

1

Remove Cylinder

The lock cylinder is removed from the door hardware.

2

Replace Pins

Internal driver pins are swapped to match a new key pattern.

3

Reinstall

The cylinder goes back in the existing hardware — no drilling or modifications.

4

New Keys Cut

New keys are cut on-site and tested. Old keys no longer work.

Texas Tenant Rekeying Rights

Texas Property Code §92.001 — Tenant Rekeying Rights

Under Texas law, tenants may request that a landlord rekey the dwelling within 7 days of a lease beginning. Landlords are generally required to rekey before a new tenant moves in. In certain circumstances, tenants may rekey themselves if the landlord fails to act — but must provide the landlord with a copy of the new key.

This is a general summary. Consult the Texas Property Code or a licensed attorney for advice specific to your situation.
