Why Professional Installation Matters

Even the best Grade 1 deadbolt provides minimal protection if improperly installed. Common installation mistakes include:

  • Weak strike plate mounting: Standard 3/4″ screws pull out under kick force. We install 3″ screws into wall studs.
  • Misaligned bolt: A bolt not fully engaging the strike plate hole leaves gaps. We align for the full 1″ throw.
  • Incorrect bore hole size: 2-1/8″ is standard — too small causes binding; too large allows cylinder manipulation.
  • No door reinforcement: Weak door jambs split under kick force. We assess and recommend steel reinforcement kits when needed.

Installation Process

1

Assess Door & Backset

Measure door thickness, stile width, and backset (2-3/8″ or 2-3/4″). Confirm correct hardware for your door prep.

2

Remove Old Hardware

Existing deadbolt removed. Door edge and frame inspected for damage, rot, or weakness.

3

Install Deadbolt & Reinforce Strike Plate

Deadbolt installed per manufacturer spec. Strike plate mortised and fastened with 3″ structural screws for kick resistance.

4

Test & Provide Keys

Full bolt throw tested, key operation confirmed smooth. Two keys provided. Keyed-alike to existing locks available on request.

Deadbolt Installation FAQ

Deadbolt installation costs $65–$150 for labor. Hardware ranges from $30 (Grade 2) to $200+ (Grade 1 high-security). A complete installed Grade 1 deadbolt runs $95–$250 total. We provide firm quotes before starting work.

ANSI Grade 1 withstands 10 hammer strikes at 75 ft-lbs and 250,000 cycles — the standard for exterior residential doors. Grade 2 withstands 5 strikes and 150,000 cycles — for light commercial or interior use. Grade 3 is interior-only and should never be used on exterior doors.

Single cylinder (key outside, thumbturn inside) is standard and allows fast interior egress. Double cylinder (keyed both sides) is for doors with nearby glass panels to prevent break-and-reach entry. Always check local fire codes — double cylinder can slow emergency egress.

Yes for most solid wood and metal doors with adequate stile width (2.25″+). Very narrow stile doors, glass doors, or aluminum storefront doors may require special hardware. We assess your specific door before recommending hardware.

We install Schlage B60N/B62N, Kwikset 985/980, Medeco Maxum, Mul-T-Lock MT5+, Baldwin, Yale, and Weiser. For most Dallas homes we recommend Schlage B60N — Grade 1, pick-resistant, excellent value at $50–$80 for hardware.

Replacing an existing deadbolt takes 15–30 minutes. New installation (drilling required) takes 45–60 minutes including the bore hole, strike plate mortise, and alignment testing. We bring all tools and most popular hardware on our service vehicles.
