Do Not Duplicate (DND) Keys — What You Need to Know

Many keys are stamped "Do Not Duplicate" — but this stamp carries no legal weight in Texas or anywhere in the US. Any licensed locksmith can legally copy a DND key. The stamp is a voluntary request, not a legal restriction.

For true key control protection, the answer is a patented restricted keyway system:

  • Medeco: Patented sidebar mechanism — requires factory authorization AND a unique angle-cut that standard machines can't produce
  • Mul-T-Lock: Pin-in-pin cylinder with restricted blanks — controlled distribution through authorized dealers only
  • Schlage Everest/Primus: Secondary locking element — requires dealer authorization to duplicate
  • ASSA Abloy Protec2: Side locking bar, patent-protected, controlled distribution

These systems mean unauthorized copies literally cannot be made — the blank key material isn't available without factory authorization.

When to Make Spare Keys

The best time to make spare keys is before you need them. Reasons to have spares cut now:

  • Lockouts average $65–$150 — a $5 spare key prevents this
  • Family members, trusted neighbors, or pet sitters may need access
  • Keys wear over time — a spare made from the original is more accurate than one made from a worn copy
  • Moving into a new home? Rekey and make fresh spares immediately
