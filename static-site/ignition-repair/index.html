Why Your Ignition Won't Turn — Diagnosis Guide

📌 Check #1: Steering Wheel Lock

Most common cause. The steering column lock engages when you remove the key with the wheel turned. Solution: gently rock the steering wheel left and right while simultaneously turning the ignition key. This releases the lock. Takes 5 seconds.

📌 Check #2: Gear Selector Position

Automatic transmissions require the selector to be fully in Park (not just Neutral) before the ignition will turn to Start or the key will release. Push the gear selector firmly into Park and try again.

📌 Check #3: Key Condition

A worn key blade may not engage tumblers properly. Try a spare key if available. If the spare works but the primary doesn't, you need a new key cut — not ignition repair.

📌 Check #4: Battery State

Very low battery voltage can prevent the ignition switch from functioning correctly. If your car is completely dead, the key release solenoid may not activate. Jump-start or charge first, then test the ignition.

📌 Check #5: Worn Tumblers

After 100,000+ key cycles, the tumbler pins inside the cylinder wear down. Symptoms: key requires precise angle/pressure, works intermittently, or won't turn at all. This requires cylinder replacement — not fixable by lubrication.
