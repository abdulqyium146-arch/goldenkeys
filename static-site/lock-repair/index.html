Lock Repair FAQ

Lock repair costs $55–$150 for most residential repairs. Commercial lock repair runs $75–$250. We provide a firm quote before starting work. When replacement is more cost-effective than repair, we'll advise you honestly.

Repair when: the lock is high-quality and the issue is minor (worn springs, misalignment, lubrication). Replace when: the lock was compromised in a break-in attempt, the cylinder is deeply worn, or it's low-grade hardware that isn't worth repairing. We give honest recommendations.

Yes. A lock that won't turn is usually caused by a worn key, worn internal tumblers, a seized cylinder from lack of lubrication, or door misalignment causing bolt binding. We diagnose and repair the root cause rather than just applying temporary lubrication.

Common causes: dry cylinder tumblers (needs graphite or Teflon lubricant), worn key not engaging tumblers correctly, door frame settling causing misalignment, or debris in the cylinder. Most sticky lock issues are repaired in under 30 minutes.

We assess post-break-in damage and repair or replace as needed. After forced entry, we check the cylinder, strike plate, bolt, and door frame. We typically recommend replacing the lock cylinder after any security breach — a compromised cylinder may have internal damage not visible externally.
